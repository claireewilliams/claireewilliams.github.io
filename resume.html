The vast majority of microorganisms in the world are unable to go in a standard laboratory petri dish. Uncultured or "not-yet-cultured" microorganisms are a barrier to microbiology, as having an organism in culture allows microbiologists to study and manipulate an organism's physiology. Novel cultivation approaches like in situ approaches enable access to these not yet cultivated organisms. Using a culture collection from the high arctic in Greenland, we explored the effectiveness of varying types of novel cultivation approaches.
